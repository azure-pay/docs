This is the production integration path.

## Flow

1. Customer chooses products in your store.
2. Your backend creates an internal order in `pending_payment`.
3. Your backend calls VoucherPay to create a checkout session.
4. Your frontend mounts the VoucherPay widget.
5. VoucherPay handles terms and hosted card entry.
6. VoucherPay captures payment and issues a voucher.
7. VoucherPay sends `payment.captured` to your webhook endpoint.
8. Your backend verifies the webhook and marks the order paid.
9. Your operations team can reconcile the payment and voucher in the merchant portal.

## Store database recommendation

Store at least:

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `order_id` | Your immutable reference and idempotency key. |
| `voucherpay_session_id` | Link to VoucherPay session. |
| `voucher_reference` | Voucher issued after capture, when present. |
| `payment_status` | Your local order/payment state. |
| `last_webhook_event_id` | Helpful for support and duplicate handling. |

## State mapping

| VoucherPay signal | Suggested store action |
| --- | --- |
| Checkout session created | Keep order `pending_payment`. |
| Widget `onProcessing` | Show waiting state to customer. |
| Widget `onSuccess` | Show success screen, but wait for backend confirmation for fulfillment. |
| `payment.captured` webhook | Mark order paid and release fulfillment. |
| `payment.failed` webhook | Mark payment failed or allow retry. |
| Timeout/no webhook | Reconcile by session ID before manual action. |

## Why webhooks are required

The customer can close the browser before redirect. A redirect can be blocked. A network call can time out after the acquirer captured payment.

For that reason, the webhook is the durable payment confirmation path.
