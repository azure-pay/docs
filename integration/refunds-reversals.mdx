---
title: "Refunds and reversals"
description: "Understand the difference between same-day reversals and post-settlement refunds."
---

VoucherPay supports post-payment money movement through controlled merchant and admin workflows.

## Reversal

A reversal is a same-day void before settlement. Use it when the original payment should be cancelled before it settles.

Typical use cases:

- customer abandons immediately after payment;
- duplicate payment caught the same day;
- merchant cannot fulfil before settlement.

## Refund

A refund is used after settlement. Depending on acquirer rules, refunds can be full or partial.

Typical use cases:

- product returned;
- partial order cancelled;
- customer service adjustment.

## Idempotency

Refund and reversal requests must use an `Idempotency-Key`. Use a stable key derived from your internal operation ID:

```text
refund-ORDER-1234-1
reverse-ORDER-1234
```

Never retry money-moving calls without the same idempotency key.

## Operational note

Refunds, reversals, withdrawals, and chargebacks affect merchant balances and audit trails. Use webhooks and the merchant portal to reconcile the final state.
