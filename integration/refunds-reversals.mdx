---
title: "Refunds and reversals"
description: "Understand post-payment operations and when to use refunds or reversals."
---

Refunds and reversals affect balances, vouchers, and audit history. Treat them as operational money movement, not frontend checkout actions.

## Reversal

A reversal is a same-day void before settlement. Use it when the original payment should be cancelled before it settles.

Examples:

- duplicate payment caught immediately;
- order cannot be fulfilled before settlement;
- customer cancels shortly after payment.

## Refund

A refund is used after settlement or when a reversal is no longer available.

Examples:

- returned item;
- partial cancellation;
- customer support adjustment.

## Integration guidance

Do not expose refund or reversal operations directly to the browser.

Today, refund/reversal operations are controlled through authenticated merchant/admin workflows. The server SDK contains a refund helper for internal/operator-authenticated paths, but merchants should not treat it as a normal secret-key checkout API until VoucherPay confirms the public contract for your account.

## Idempotency

Every refund or reversal operation must use an idempotency key.

Recommended examples:

```text
refund:ORDER-1234:1
reverse:ORDER-1234
```

## Webhooks and reconciliation

After a refund or reversal, reconcile using:

- merchant portal payment detail;
- voucher state;
- webhook events enabled for your account;
- support/export records when needed.
