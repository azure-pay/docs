---
title: "How VoucherPay works"
description: "The parties, payment objects, and voucher lifecycle behind a VoucherPay checkout."
---

VoucherPay sits between your store, the acquirer, and your merchant portal.

## Parties

| Party | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Cardholder | The customer buying from your store. |
| Merchant | Your business. You own the customer relationship and the order. |
| VoucherPay | Creates checkout sessions, tracks payment state, issues vouchers, and manages merchant settlement workflows. |
| Acquirer | Processes the card payment and 3DS challenge through a hosted payment surface. |

## Main objects

| Object | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Checkout session | A payment attempt created for one store order. |
| Client secret | Browser-safe session secret used by the widget to read public status. |
| Voucher | The receipt-like record issued after successful capture. |
| Webhook event | A signed notification from VoucherPay to your backend. |
| Payout destination | Your approved bank or wallet destination for withdrawals. |
| Withdrawal | A merchant request to move available funds out of VoucherPay. |

## Voucher lifecycle

The normal happy path is:

1. `CREATED` or `PENDING_GATES`
2. `CHECKOUT_IN_PROGRESS`
3. `CAPTURED`
4. voucher issued
5. voucher funded and allocated to merchant books
6. available for withdrawal after any configured controls

Operational controls such as settlement delay, reserve, chargeback holds, and payout approval can affect when funds become withdrawable.
