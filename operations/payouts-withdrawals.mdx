---
title: "Payouts and withdrawals"
description: "Understand how captured payments become available and how withdrawals are requested."
---

Captured payments do not always become immediately withdrawable. VoucherPay can apply controls before funds are available.

## Balance states

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Captured | Payment succeeded and voucher was issued. |
| On merchant books | Voucher value has moved to the merchant ledger. |
| Locked or held | Value is temporarily blocked by settlement delay, reserve, risk review, or chargeback control. |
| Available | Value can be included in a withdrawal request. |
| Withdrawn | Withdrawal has completed. |

## Payout destinations

Before withdrawing, add a payout destination and wait for approval.

Supported destination types depend on your account setup. The portal may support bank accounts and wallet destinations.

## Withdrawal flow

1. Choose an active payout destination.
2. Select available value.
3. Submit the withdrawal request.
4. VoucherPay reviews and processes the withdrawal.
5. Final state is visible in the portal and sent by webhook.

## Why funds may be locked

Funds can be locked because of:

- settlement delay;
- rolling reserve;
- chargeback hold;
- missing payout destination approval;
- operational review.

Your commercial agreement controls which rules apply in production.
